	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" ><i>United States Statutes at Large</i></h1>

<p>The <i><b>United States Statutes at Large</b></i>, commonly referred to as the <i><b>Statutes at Large</b></i> and abbreviated <b>Stat.</b>, are an official record of <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Congress" title="Act of Congress">Acts of Congress</a> and <a href="/wiki/Concurrent_resolution" title="Concurrent resolution">concurrent resolutions</a> passed by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a>. Each act and resolution of Congress is originally published as a <a href="/wiki/Slip_law" title="Slip law">slip law</a>, which is classified as either <a href="/wiki/Public_bill" class="mw-redirect" title="Public bill">public law</a> (abbreviated Pub.L.) or <a href="/wiki/Private_bill" class="mw-redirect" title="Private bill">private law</a> (Pvt.L.), and designated and numbered accordingly. At the end of a Congressional session, the statutes enacted during that session are compiled into bound books, known as "session law" publications. The session law publication for U.S. Federal statutes is called the <i>United States Statutes at Large</i>. In that publication, the public laws and private laws are numbered and organized in chronological order.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> U.S. Federal statutes are published in a three-part process, consisting of slip laws, session laws (<i>Statutes at Large</i>), and <a href="/wiki/Codification_(law)" title="Codification (law)">codification</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/United_States_Code" title="United States Code">United States Code</a></i>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Codification">Codification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Statutes_at_Large&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Codification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Large portions of public laws are enacted as amendments to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Code" title="United States Code">United States Code</a>. Once enacted into law, an Act will be published in the <i>Statutes at Large</i> and will add to, modify, or delete some part of the United States Code. Provisions of a public law that contain only enacting clauses, effective dates, and similar matters are not generally <a href="/wiki/Codification_(law)" title="Codification (law)">codified</a>. Private laws also are not generally codified.
</p><p>Some portions of the United States Code have been enacted as <a href="/wiki/Positive_law" title="Positive law">positive law</a> and other portions have not been so enacted. In case of a conflict between the text of the <i>Statutes at Large</i> and the text of a provision of the United States Code that has not been enacted as positive law, the text of the <i>Statutes at Large</i> takes precedence.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Statutes_at_Large&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:US_Statutes_at_Large.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/US_Statutes_at_Large.jpg/220px-US_Statutes_at_Large.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="178" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/US_Statutes_at_Large.jpg/330px-US_Statutes_at_Large.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/US_Statutes_at_Large.jpg/440px-US_Statutes_at_Large.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2370" data-file-height="1914" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:US_Statutes_at_Large.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A few volumes of the <i>Statutes at Large</i></div></div></div>
<p>Publication of the <i>United States Statutes at Large</i> began in 1845 by the private firm of <a href="/wiki/Little,_Brown_and_Company" title="Little, Brown and Company">Little, Brown and Company</a> under authority of a <a href="/wiki/Joint_resolution_of_Congress" class="mw-redirect" title="Joint resolution of Congress">joint resolution of Congress</a>. During Little, Brown and Company's time as publisher, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Peters_(reporter)" title="Richard Peters (reporter)">Richard Peters</a> (Volumes 1–8), <a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Minot_(attorney)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="George Minot (attorney) (page does not exist)">George Minot</a> (Volumes 9–11), and <a href="/wiki/George_P._Sanger" title="George P. Sanger">George P. Sanger</a> (Volumes 11–17) served as editors.
</p><p>In 1874, Congress transferred the authority to publish the <i>Statutes at Large</i> to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Government_Publishing_Office" title="United States Government Publishing Office">Government Printing Office</a> under the direction of the Secretary of State.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Public_Law_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Law (United States)"><abbr title="Public Law (United States)">Pub.L.</abbr></a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://uslaw.link/citation/us-law/public/80/278">80–278</a>, 61 Stat. 633, was enacted July 30, 1947 and directed the Secretary of State to compile, edit, index, and publish the <i>Statutes at Large</i>. <a href="/wiki/Public_Law_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Law (United States)"><abbr title="Public Law (United States)">Pub.L.</abbr></a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://uslaw.link/citation/us-law/public/81/821">81–821</a>, 64 Stat. 980, was enacted September 23, 1950 and directed the <a href="/wiki/Administrator_of_General_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Administrator of General Services">Administrator of General Services</a> to compile, edit, index, and publish the <i>Statutes at Large</i>. Since 1985 the <i>Statutes at Large</i> have been prepared and published by the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Federal_Register" title="Office of the Federal Register">Office of the Federal Register</a> (OFR) of the <a href="/wiki/National_Archives_and_Records_Administration" title="National Archives and Records Administration">National Archives and Records Administration</a> (NARA).<sup id="cite_ref-USGPO-About_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGPO-About-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Until 1948, all <a href="/wiki/Treaty" title="Treaty">treaties</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="International agreement">international agreements</a> approved by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> were also published in the set, but these now appear in a publication titled <a href="/wiki/United_States_Treaties_and_Other_International_Agreements" title="United States Treaties and Other International Agreements">United States Treaties and Other International Agreements</a>, abbreviated <b>U.S.T.</b> In addition, the <i>Statutes at Large</i> includes the text of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence" title="United States Declaration of Independence">Declaration of Independence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Articles_of_Confederation" title="Articles of Confederation">Articles of Confederation</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Constitution">Constitution</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_amendments_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="List of amendments to the United States Constitution">amendments to the Constitution</a>, treaties with <a href="/wiki/Tribal_sovereignty_in_the_United_States" title="Tribal sovereignty in the United States">Native American nations</a> and foreign nations, and <a href="/wiki/Presidential_proclamation" class="mw-redirect" title="Presidential proclamation">presidential proclamations</a>.
</p><p>Sometimes very large or long Acts of Congress are published as their own "appendix" volume of the <i>Statutes at Large</i>. For example, the <a href="/wiki/Internal_Revenue_Code_of_1954" class="mw-redirect" title="Internal Revenue Code of 1954">Internal Revenue Code of 1954</a> was published as volume 68A of the <i>Statutes at Large</i> (68A&#160;<a class="mw-selflink selflink">Stat.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/STATUTE-68/pdf/STATUTE-68A-Pg3.pdf">3</a>).
</p>
