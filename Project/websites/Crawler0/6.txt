<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 205px;">
<a href="/wiki/File:Dylan2_Spectrum.jpg" class="image" title="Dylan&#39;s band in Oslo on March 30, 2007, one of hundreds of concerts at which he has performed &quot;Watching the River Flow&quot;"><img alt="Dylan&#39;s band in Oslo on March 30, 2007, one of hundreds of concerts at which he has performed &quot;Watching the River Flow&quot;" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Dylan2_Spectrum.jpg/205px-Dylan2_Spectrum.jpg" decoding="async" width="205" height="95" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Dylan2_Spectrum.jpg/308px-Dylan2_Spectrum.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Dylan2_Spectrum.jpg/410px-Dylan2_Spectrum.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="465" /></a><div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Dylan's band in Oslo on March 30, 2007, one of hundreds of concerts at which he has performed "Watching the River Flow"</div></div>

<p>"<b><a href="/wiki/Watching_the_River_Flow" title="Watching the River Flow">Watching the River Flow</a></b>" is a <a href="/wiki/Blues_rock" title="Blues rock">blues rock</a> song by American singer <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a>, released as a single on June&#160;3, 1971. Produced by <a href="/wiki/Leon_Russell" title="Leon Russell">Leon Russell</a>, it was written and recorded during a session in March&#160;1971 at <a href="/wiki/Blue_Rock_Studio" title="Blue Rock Studio">Blue Rock Studio</a> in New York City. The collaboration with Russell formed in part through Dylan's desire for a new sound, after a period of immersion in <a href="/wiki/Country_rock" title="Country rock">country rock</a> music. The song has been interpreted as Dylan's account of his writer's block in the early 1970s, and his wish to deliver less politically engaged material and find a new balance between public and private life. A minor hit in some countries, "Watching the River Flow" was included on the 1971 <i><a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan%27s_Greatest_Hits_Vol._II" title="Bob Dylan&#39;s Greatest Hits Vol. II">Bob Dylan's Greatest Hits Vol.&#160;II</a></i> and other Dylan compilation albums. The song has been covered by many artists, including a recording by five current and former <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones</a> that was part of a tribute project for pianist <a href="/wiki/Ian_Stewart_(musician)" title="Ian Stewart (musician)">Ian Stewart</a>. (<b><a href="/wiki/Watching_the_River_Flow" title="Watching the River Flow">Full&#160;article...</a></b>)

<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Double-banded_plover" title="Double-banded plover">double-banded plover</a></b> (<i>Charadrius bicinctus</i>) is a species of bird in the <a href="/wiki/Plover" title="Plover">plover</a> family native to New Zealand. During the winter and the spring, it has a dark, greyish-brown back with a distinctive white chest, a thin band of black below the neck running across the chest, and a thicker brown band below. Outside the breeding season, the double-banding is lost; this photograph, taken in March, shows a double-banded plover in non-breeding plumage at <a href="/wiki/Boat_Harbour,_New_South_Wales" title="Boat Harbour, New South Wales">Boat Harbour</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, Australia.
