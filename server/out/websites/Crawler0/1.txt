<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 114px;">
<a href="/wiki/File:Johnny_Owen_Merthyr_Boxer_by_Aberdare_Blog.jpg" class="image" title="Statue of Owen in Merthyr Tydfil"><img alt="Statue of Owen in Merthyr Tydfil" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Johnny_Owen_Merthyr_Boxer_by_Aberdare_Blog.jpg/114px-Johnny_Owen_Merthyr_Boxer_by_Aberdare_Blog.jpg" decoding="async" width="114" height="171" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Johnny_Owen_Merthyr_Boxer_by_Aberdare_Blog.jpg/171px-Johnny_Owen_Merthyr_Boxer_by_Aberdare_Blog.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Johnny_Owen_Merthyr_Boxer_by_Aberdare_Blog.jpg/228px-Johnny_Owen_Merthyr_Boxer_by_Aberdare_Blog.jpg 2x" data-file-width="240" data-file-height="360" /></a><div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Statue of Owen in Merthyr Tydfil</div></div>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Johnny_Owen" title="Johnny Owen">Johnny Owen</a></b> (1956â€“1980) was a Welsh <a href="/wiki/Professional_boxing" title="Professional boxing">professional boxer</a>. He won his first title, the <a href="/wiki/Bantamweight" title="Bantamweight">bantamweight</a> title for the <a href="/wiki/British_Boxing_Board_of_Control#Councils" title="British Boxing Board of Control">Welsh Area</a>, in March&#160;1977, seven months after turning professional. Later in 1977, Owen defeated <a href="/wiki/Paddy_Maguire_(boxer)" title="Paddy Maguire (boxer)">Paddy Maguire</a> for the <a href="/wiki/List_of_British_bantamweight_boxing_champions" title="List of British bantamweight boxing champions">British bantamweight title</a>, becoming the first Welshman in more than 60 years to hold the <a href="/wiki/Championship_belt" title="Championship belt">belt</a>. He defeated <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ferreri" title="Paul Ferreri">Paul Ferreri</a> for the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Commonwealth_Boxing_Council_champions#Bantamweight" title="List of Commonwealth Boxing Council champions">Commonwealth bantamweight title</a> but was controversially beaten by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Francisco_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Juan Francisco RodrÃ­guez">Juan Francisco RodrÃ­guez</a> for the <a href="/wiki/List_of_European_Boxing_Union_champions#Bantamweight" title="List of European Boxing Union champions">European title</a>. He later defeated RodrÃ­guez in a rematch. Owen challenged <a href="/wiki/World_Boxing_Council" title="World Boxing Council">World Boxing Council</a> (WBC) champion <a href="/wiki/Lupe_Pintor" title="Lupe Pintor">Lupe Pintor</a> for his <a href="/wiki/List_of_WBC_world_champions#Bantamweight" title="List of WBC world champions">world bantamweight title</a> on 19&#160;September&#160;1980, losing in a twelfth-round <a href="/wiki/Knockout" title="Knockout">knockout</a>, leaving the ring on a stretcher. He never regained consciousness, dying seven weeks later at age 24. Owen possessed a professional career record of 25 wins (11 by knockout), 1 draw and 2 defeats. A statue <i>(pictured)</i> commemorating his life and career stands in his home town of <a href="/wiki/Merthyr_Tydfil" title="Merthyr Tydfil">Merthyr Tydfil</a>.
